# Atlas-HEC v2.1: Self-Improving Systems via Trajectory-Based Inheritance

**Abstract**

We present Atlas-HEC v2.1, a system demonstrating three sequential capabilities in self-improving AI: (1) single-task self-improvement through inheritance mechanisms (L4), (2) cross-task transfer of improvement via structured inheritance packages (L5), and (3) learned policy selection that matches human-engineered heuristics (L6). 

Operating under the "Sole Reference Principle"—where progress is measured against the system's own historical trajectory rather than external benchmarks—we validate these capabilities through 190+ execution windows with comprehensive statistical controls. L4 establishes an 18.7pp control gap over baseline. L5 demonstrates positive transfer across all 6 task pairs (Math, Code, Planning) with a mean transfer gap of 9.34pp and a discoverable source-suitability hierarchy (Code > Math > Planning). L6 shows that a lightweight learned policy can match hand-coded heuristics for source selection (Tier 2 Match, 3/3 runs reproducible).

A documented protocol evolution—from circuit-breaker v1.0 (relative thresholds) to v2.0 (absolute thresholds) following a rule-design failure—demonstrates the system's commitment to audited, self-correcting methodology. All claims are scoped: validated within the tested task family, with explicit limitations on external generalization. 

**Key Contributions**: (1) Validated trajectory-based inheritance for self-improvement, (2) Demonstrated cross-task viability with directional structure, (3) Showed learned policy can match heuristic performance, (4) Established protocol for self-auditing experimental systems.

**Keywords**: self-improving AI, meta-learning, transfer learning, experimental methodology, trajectory-based systems
